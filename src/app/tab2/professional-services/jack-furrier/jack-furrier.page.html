Story time about Jack Furriers. On a road trip back to Tucson from San Diego, I was just outside of Yuma when a semi traveling in front of me caught on fire. The driver hit his breaks and the trailer separated from the cab. The trailer turned and slide to a stop, blocking all three lanes.

The rig was on fire and had stopped right in front of me. I was in the far-right lane so in order to avoid smashing into the rig, I swerved off the freeway. I headed right into the raven. My Jeep SUV handled the rough road very well and I was able to ride along the side of the freeway, in the ditch, getting past the semi. I headed to the front of the burning vehicle, which was melting into the pavement, to make sure no one was injured. I called 911 and went on my way. The next day I left to pick up clients only to discover that I had four flat tires! The tires were full of railroad spikes. Go figure! I had the car towed to Jack Furriers and they fixed all of the tires. The manager would not let me pay for the repairs. I told him that I didn’t purchase the tires from him, and he still wouldn’t take any money. He said, “remember that we are here for you, anytime you need us”. We have. We have remained loyal Jack Furrier customers ever since.
