Lake Roosevelt, found in the Tonto National Forest, is a very modern and well-equipped campground. There are full 50 / 30 amp hookups for your RVâ€™s and trailers, complete with oversize spaces for easy backing-up of your RV. Every site features a concrete patio and a fire ring (and often grills) for warmth and cooking. There is also general store to pick up an last-minute supplies.

The design of the campsite is truly visionary. Rather than have everyone huddle around the lake so they can get a good view, the sites are set up on a vertical terrace system (similar to stadium seating) that gives everyone an unobstructed view of the natural wonder that is Theodore Roosevelt Lake.

What To Do at Lake Roosevelt?

Our days were spent on a pontoon boat that we rented from the fantastic marina. This boat was oversized for our small family and could accommodate more people, making it the perfect option for a retreat with a group of friends. The best thing is dogs are allowed with you, and Baxter and Bailer certainly enjoyed staring at the fish playing in the water. When it was time to nap, the boat was covered with plush couches, perfect for a quick snuggle.

Another great option, besides the pontoon boats, would be the specially designed horse campground. You get your own corral and ramada, with bench seating at every site. You can pull up with your horse trailer, move your horse to the corral, then set up your camp. Once everything is ready, you can then set out on the many manicured horse trails near the lake.
